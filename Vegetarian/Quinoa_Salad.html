Quinoa

Quinoa salad is a healthy and versatile dish that is easy to make and packed with nutrients. It typically consists of cooked quinoa mixed with fresh vegetables, herbs, and a flavorful dressing, making it a great option for a light lunch or a side dish. Quinoa is a high-protein and gluten-free seed that is rich in fiber, vitamins, and minerals, and it provides a nutty and satisfying base for the salad.

The vegetables and herbs add texture, flavor, and color to the dish, and the dressing ties everything together with its tangy and savory taste. Quinoa salads are a popular choice for vegetarians and vegans, and they can be customized in countless ways to suit individual preferences and dietary needs.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup uncooked quinoa
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 can chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, diced
  • 1 small red onion, diced
  • 1 cucumber, diced
  • 1/2 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1/4 cup fresh mint, chopped
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 3 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Step By Step

  1. Rinse the quinoa thoroughly in a fine-mesh strainer.
  2. In a medium saucepan, bring the quinoa and water to a boil. Reduce the heat to low and simmer, covered, for about 15 minutes, or until the water has been absorbed.
  3. Remove the saucepan from the heat and let the quinoa sit, covered, for about 5 minutes.
  4. Fluff the quinoa with a fork and transfer it to a large mixing bowl.
  5. Add the chickpeas, red and yellow bell peppers, red onion, cucumber, parsley, and mint to the bowl with the quinoa.
  6. In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, salt, and pepper.
  7. Pour the dressing over the quinoa salad and toss to combine.
  8. Taste and adjust the seasoning as needed.
  9. Chill the salad for at least 30 minutes before serving to allow the flavors to meld together.

NUTRITION FACTS

Quinoa : a good source of protein, dietary fiber, and several essential nutrients, including magnesium, phosphorus, and manganese.

Chickpeas: an excellent source of protein, dietary fiber, and several essential nutrients, including folate, iron, and manganese.

Lemon juice: a good source of vitamin C and citric acid, which can help improve digestion.

Bell peppers: a good source of vitamin C and several other vitamins and minerals.

Red onion: a good source of dietary fiber and several essential nutrients, including vitamin C and manganese.

Cucumber: low in calories and a good source of vitamin K and several other vitamins and minerals.

Olive oil: a healthy fat that's rich in monounsaturated fatty acids and antioxidants.
