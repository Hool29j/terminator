Ratatouille

Ratatouille is a classic French vegetable stew that originated in the Provence region of France. It features a variety of vegetables, including eggplant, zucchini, bell peppers, onions, and tomatoes, cooked with olive oil and flavorful herbs and spices.

Ratatouille is a hearty and rustic dish that can be served as a side dish or a main course, either hot or cold. It's a great way to enjoy fresh and healthy vegetables and is often accompanied by crusty bread or pasta.

Ingredients

  • 1 large eggplant
  • 2 zucchinis
  • 2 bell peppers (red, yellow, or green)
  • 2 onions
  • 4 cloves of garlic
  • 4 large tomatoes
  • 1/4 cup of olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh basil leaves, for garnish

Step By Step

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cut the eggplant, zucchinis, bell peppers, and onions into small cubes. Crush the garlic cloves and roughly chop the tomatoes.
  3. Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the onions and garlic and sauté until the onions are translucent.
  4. Add the eggplant and cook for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the eggplant is slightly softened.
  5. Add the zucchinis and bell peppers and continue cooking for another 5-7 minutes until the vegetables are slightly softened.
  6. Add the chopped tomatoes to the skillet and stir everything together. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  7. Transfer the mixture to a large baking dish and bake for 30-40 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender and lightly browned.
  8. Once the Ratatouille is done, remove it from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes.
  9. Garnish with fresh basil leaves and serve hot.
