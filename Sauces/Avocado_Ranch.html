Avocado Ranch

Avocado ranch is a type of dressing or sauce made by combining ranch dressing with mashed avocado. It is a creamy, tangy and flavorful condiment that is commonly used as a dip or a salad dressing. The avocado adds a smooth texture and a mild, buttery flavor to the classic ranch dressing.

Some variations of avocado ranch may include additional ingredients such as lime juice, garlic, cilantro, or jalapeño peppers to enhance its taste and add a spicy kick. Avocado ranch is a popular condiment in American cuisine, particularly as a dip for vegetables, chips, or chicken wings.

Ingredients

  • 500g bread flour
  • 10g salt
  • 7g active dry yeast
  • 400ml lukewarm water
  • 2 thsp olive oil

Step By Step

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine the bread flour, salt, and yeast. Mix well.
  2. Add the lukewarm water and olive oil to the dry ingredients. Mix until a sticky dough forms.
  3. Knead the dough by hand or with a mixer until it becomes smooth and elastic. The dough will still be slightly sticky, but it should not stick to your fingers.
  4. Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl and cover it with a damp cloth. Let it rise in a warm, draft-free place for 1-2 hours, or until it has doubled in size.
  5. Preheat your oven to 425°F (218°C). If you have a baking stone, place it in the oven to preheat as well.
  6. Transfer the dough to a floured surface and shape it into a long, rectangular loaf. It should be about 1 inch thick.
  7. Transfer the loaf to a baking sheet lined with parchment paper or a floured couche.
  8. Let the dough rest for 15-20 minutes. This will help it develop a crisp crust.
  9. Use a sharp knife or razor blade to make several diagonal slashes on the top of the loaf.
  10. Place the baking sheet in the oven, and bake the bread for 25-30 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the bread sounds hollow when tapped on the bottom.
  11. Let the bread cool on a wire rack before slicing and serving.
