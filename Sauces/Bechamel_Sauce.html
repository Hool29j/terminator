Béchamel Sauce

Béchamel sauce, also known as white sauce, is a French sauce made from a roux (a mixture of flour and butter) and milk. The roux is cooked until it is a pale, golden color, and then milk is gradually added, while constantly whisking, until the sauce thickens. Béchamel sauce is a creamy and versatile sauce that can be used as a base for many other sauces, such as cheese sauce, or as a component in dishes such as lasagna or macaroni and cheese.

It can also be flavored with ingredients such as garlic, nutmeg, or herbs to enhance its taste. Béchamel sauce is a fundamental sauce in French cuisine and is also used in many other cuisines around the world.

Ingredients

  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 4 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 4 cups whole milk
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • Pinch of ground nutmeg (optional)

Step By Step

  1. In a medium saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat until it begins to foam.
  2. Add the flour and whisk continuously for about 2 minutes, or until the mixture turns a light golden color
  3. Gradually pour in the milk, whisking constantly to prevent lumps from forming.
  4. Continue to whisk the sauce over medium heat until it begins to thicken and comes to a gentle simmer.
  5. Reduce the heat to low and simmer the sauce for about 10-15 minutes, or until it has thickened enough to coat the back of a spoon.
  6. Remove the saucepan from the heat and stir in the salt, black pepper, and nutmeg (if using).
  7. Taste the sauce and adjust the seasoning as needed.
