espagnole Sauce

Espagnole sauce, also known as brown sauce, is a classic French sauce made from a base of browned beef or veal bones, vegetables, and aromatics, such as onion, carrot, and celery, that are simmered with tomato paste and red wine. The resulting sauce is then strained and thickened with a roux (a mixture of flour and fat) to create a rich and flavorful sauce.

Espagnole sauce is a complex and versatile sauce that can be used as a base for many other sauces, such as demi-glace, or as a component in dishes such as stews or braised meats. It is a fundamental sauce in French cuisine and is also used in many other cuisines around the world.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups beef or veal stock
  • 2 tbsp butter/li>
  • 2 tbsp flour
  • 1/2 onion, chopped
  • 1 carrot, chopped
  • 1 celery stalk, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1/2 cup tomato puree
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 2 sprigs of thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Step By Step

  1. Melt the butter in a saucepan over medium heat. Add the chopped onion, carrot, celery, and garlic, and sauté until the vegetables are soft and translucent.
  2. Add the flour to the saucepan and stir constantly for about 2 minutes, or until the mixture has turned a light brown color.
  3. Gradually pour in the beef or veal stock, whisking continuously to avoid lumps from forming.
  4. Add the tomato puree, bay leaf, and thyme sprigs to the saucepan.Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and let it simmer for 45 minutes to 1 hour, or until the sauce has thickened and reduced by about half.
  5. Strain the sauce through a fine mesh strainer, pressing on the vegetables to extract as much liquid as possible. Discard the solids.
  6. Return the sauce to the saucepan and season with salt and pepper to taste. Simmer the sauce for an additional 10-15 minutes to let the flavors meld together.
