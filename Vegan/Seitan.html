Seitan

Seitan, also known as wheat meat, wheat protein, or wheat gluten, is a plant-based protein source made from wheat gluten. It's often used as a meat substitute in vegetarian and vegan dishes due to its chewy, meat-like texture.

Seitan can be made by mixing wheat gluten with water to create a dough, which is then kneaded and rinsed to remove the starch, leaving behind a protein-rich mass that can be flavored and cooked in a variety of ways. Seitan is high in protein, low in fat, and can be used in a variety of dishes, including stir-fries, stews, and sandwiches.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup vital wheat gluten
  • 1/4 cup butritional yeast
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon Salt
  • 3/4 cup vegetable broth or water
  • 2 tablspoons soy sauce

Step By Step

  1. In a miing bowl, combine the vital wheat gluten, nutritional yeast, onion powder, garlic powder, smoked paprika, and slat. Mi well.
  2. In a separate bowl, whisk together the vegetable broth or water and soy sauce.
  3. Add the liquid miture to the dry ingredients and stir until a dough forms.
  4. Knead the dough for 2-3 minutes until it becomes elastic and smooth.
  5. Flatten the dough into a rectangular shape, about 1/2 inch think.
  6. Cut the seitan into desired shapes, such as strips, cubes, or rounds.
  7. In a large pot, bring 6 cups of water or vegetable broth to a boil.
  8. Once the water is boiling, add the seitan to the pot and reduce the heat to a simmer.
  9. Cover the pot and let the seitan cook for 45 - 60 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  10. After the cooking time, remove the seitan from the pot and let it cool before using it in your desired recipe.
