Buddha Bowl

A Buddha Bowl is a plant-based meal that typically consists of a variety of grains, vegetables, and proteins. It's often served in a bowl, hence the name, and can be eaten hot or cold. The ingredients used in a Buddha Bowl can vary widely depending on personal preference, but common ingredients may include quinoa, brown rice, roasted vegetables, tofu, chickpeas, nuts, seeds, and dressing.

The idea behind a Buddha Bowl is to create a balanced and nutritious meal that is also visually appealing, and it's often used as a way to promote healthy eating habits.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup of cooked quinoa or brown rice
  • 1 cup of roasted vegetables (such as sweet potatoes, carrots, broccoli, or cauliflower)
  • 1/2 cup of cooked beans (such as black beans, chickpeas, or kidney beans)
  • 1/2 cup of sliced avocado
  • 1/4 cup of chopped nuts or seeds (such as almonds, pumpkin seeds, or sunflower seeds)
  • 1/4 cup of diced tomatoes
  • 1/4 cup of chopped cilantro
  • 1 tablespoon of olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon of lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Step By Step

  1. Cook the quinoa or brown rice according to package instructions.
  2. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). Cut the vegetables into bite-sized pieces and place them on a baking sheet. Drizzle with olive oil and sprinkle with salt and pepper. Roast in the oven for 20-25 minutes or until tender and slightly caramelized.
  3. Rinse and drain the beans and set aside.
  4. Assemble the Buddha bowl by placing the cooked quinoa or brown rice at the bottom of a bowl. Arrange the roasted vegetables, beans, avocado, chopped nuts or seeds, diced tomatoes, and chopped cilantro on top.
  5. In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, lemon juice, salt, and pepper. Drizzle the dressing over the Buddha bowl.
  6. Serve and enjoy your delicious and nutritious Buddha bowl!

NUTRITION FACTS

Cooked quinoa or brown rice (1 cup): 220-250 calories, 4-6 grams of protein, 40-45 grams of carbohydrates, 3-5 grams of fiber, and 1-3 grams of fat, along with various vitamins and minerals such as folate, iron, magnesium, and phosphorus.

Roasted vegetables (1 cup): 50-100 calories, 2-4 grams of protein, 10-20 grams of carbohydrates, 4-8 grams of fiber, and 1-4 grams of fat, along with various vitamins and minerals such as vitamin A, vitamin C, potassium, and magnesium.

Cooked beans (1/2 cup): 70-120 calories, 6-8 grams of protein, 12-20 grams of carbohydrates, 4-8 grams of fiber, and 0-2 grams of fat, along with various vitamins and minerals such as iron, potassium, and folate.

Avocado (1/2 cup sliced): 120-150 calories, 2-3 grams of protein, 7-10 grams of carbohydrates, 5-7 grams of fiber, and 10-15 grams of fat (mostly healthy monounsaturated and polyunsaturated fats), along with various vitamins and minerals such as vitamin K, vitamin C, potassium, and magnesium.

Nuts or seeds (1/4 cup): 150-200 calories, 4-8 grams of protein, 4-8 grams of carbohydrates, 2-4 grams of fiber, and 10-20 grams of fat (mostly healthy unsaturated fats), along with various vitamins and minerals such as magnesium, phosphorus, and zinc.

Cilantro (1/4 cup chopped): 1-2 calories, 0 grams of protein, 0-1 gram of carbohydrates, 0-1 gram of fiber, and 0 grams of fat, along with various vitamins and minerals such as vitamin A and vitamin C.

Olive oil (1 tablespoon): 120-130 calories, 0 grams of protein, 0 grams of carbohydrates, 0 grams of fiber, and 14 grams of fat (mostly healthy monounsaturated and polyunsaturated fats), along with various antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds.

Lemon juice (1 tablespoon): 5-10 calories, 0 grams of protein, 1-2 grams of carbohydrates, 0-1 gram of fiber, and 0 grams of fat, along with vitamin C and antioxidants.

Tomatoes (1/4 cup diced): 5-10 calories, 0-1 gram of protein, 1-2 grams of carbohydrates, 0-1 gram of fiber, and 0-1 gram of fat, along with various vitamins and minerals such as vitamin C, vitamin K, and potassium.
