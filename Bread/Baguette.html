Baguette

Baguette is a type of bread that originated in France and is now popular all over the world. It is a long, narrow loaf made from a simple dough of flour, water, yeast, and salt. The traditional French baguette is usually around 60-80 cm long and 5-6 cm in diameter, with a crisp crust and a soft, chewy interior.

Baguettes are typically baked in a very hot oven, which gives them their signature crispy exterior. They are often served sliced and used as a sandwich bread, but can also be enjoyed on their own or as an accompaniment to a variety of dishes. Baguettes are considered a staple of French cuisine and are an important part of the country's cultural heritage.

Ingredients

  • 500g all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 7g instant yeast
  • 350ml warm water

Step By Step

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, salt, sugar, and instant yeast
  2. Slowly add in the warm water, stirring with a wooden spoon until a dough forms.
  3. Knead the dough on a floured surface for 10-15 minutes, until it becomes smooth and elastic.
  4. Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl and cover with plastic wrap. Let it rest in a warm place for 1-2 hours, or until it doubles in size.
  5. Preheat your oven to 450°F (230°C).
  6. Divide the dough into three equal parts and shape each one into a long baguette shape.
  7. Place the baguettes onto a lightly floured baking sheet, cover with a clean kitchen towel, and let them rise for another 15-20 minutes.
  8. Use a sharp knife or razor blade to make 3-4 diagonal slashes on each baguette.
  9. Bake the baguettes in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes, or until golden brown and crispy.
  10. Remove the baguettes from the oven and let them cool on a wire rack.
