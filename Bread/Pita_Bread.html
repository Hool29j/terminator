Pita Bread

Pita bread is a Middle Eastern flatbread that is made with flour, water, yeast, and salt. It has a pocket in the center that can be filled with a variety of ingredients, such as falafel or hummus.

Pita bread is usually baked at a high temperature, which causes it to puff up and form the pocket. It's often served with dips or used as a wrap for sandwiches or kebabs.

Ingredients

  • 2 1/4 tsp active dry yeast
  • 1 tsp granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cups warm water (110 - 115°F)
  • 1/2 cps all-purpose flour
  • tsp salt
  • tsp olive oil

Step By Step

  1. In a small bowl, combine the yeast, sugar, and warm water. Stir to combine and let sit for 5 minutes, until the mixture becomes foamy.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt.
  3. Add the yeast mixture to the flour mixture, along with the olive oil, and mix until the dough comes together.
  4. Knead the dough on a lightly floured surface for 5-7 minutes, until it becomes smooth and elastic.
  5. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover with a damp towel, and let it rise in a warm, draft-free place for 1-2 hours, until it has doubled in size.
  6. Preheat the oven to 500°F (260°C) with a pizza stone or baking sheet inside.
  7. Punch down the dough and divide it into 8 equal portions. Roll each portion into a ball and flatten it into a disk about 1/4 inch thick.
  8. Place the disks on a lightly floured surface and let them rest for 10-15 minutes.
  9. Place the disks on the preheated pizza stone or baking sheet and bake for 5-6 minutes, until they have puffed up and are lightly browned.
  10. Remove the pita bread from the oven and let them cool for a few minutes before serving.
