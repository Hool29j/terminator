Whole Wheat Bread

Whole wheat bread is a type of bread made from whole wheat flour, which is made by milling the entire wheat kernel, including the bran and germ. This gives the bread a nuttier flavor and denser texture than bread made from refined wheat flour.

Whole wheat bread is often used for sandwiches or toast, and is a healthier alternative to white bread because it's higher in fiber and nutrients. It can be made with additional ingredients such as seeds or grains for added texture and flavor.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups whole wheat flour
  • 2 cups warm water
  • 2 tbsp honey or maple syrup
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp instant yeast
  • 1 tsp salt

Step By Step

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the whole wheat flour, instant yeast, and salt.
  2. In a separate mixing bowl, combine the warm water, honey or maple syrup, and olive oil. Stir to combine.
  3. Add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients and stir until a sticky dough forms.
  4. Transfer the dough to a floured surface and knead for about 10 minutes until the dough becomes smooth and elastic.
  5. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover with a damp cloth, and let it rise in a warm place for about 1 hour or until it doubles in size.
  6. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  7. Once the dough has risen, punch it down and shape it into a loaf. Place the loaf in a greased 9-inch by 5-inch loaf pan.
  8. Cover the pan with a damp cloth and let it rise for another 30 minutes.
  9. Bake the bread for 30 to 40 minutes, or until the bread is golden brown and sounds hollow when tapped.
  10. Remove the bread from the pan and let it cool on a wire rack before slicing and serving.
