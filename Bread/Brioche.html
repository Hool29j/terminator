Baguette

Brioche is a French bread that is enriched with butter and eggs, which gives it a tender, fluffy texture and a rich, slightly sweet flavor. It originated in the region of Normandy, but is now popular throughout France and beyond.

Brioche dough is often used to make a variety of baked goods, including rolls, pastries, and desserts. It can be eaten on its own or used as a base for sweet or savory dishes. Brioche is often associated with French cuisine and is considered a luxurious and indulgent bread.

Ingredients

  • 500g all-purpose flour
  • 10g salt
  • 60g sugar
  • 10g active dry yeast
  • 5 large eggs
  • 200g unsalted butter, at room temperature and cut into small cubes
  • 1 egg yolk beaten with 1 tablespoon of milk (for egg wash)

Step By Step

  1. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, salt, sugar, and yeast.
  2. Add the eggs, one at a time, mixing well after each addition. Keep mixing until the dough comes together and becomes smooth.
  3. Add the butter, a few cubes at a time, and mix until all the butter is incorporated and the dough is smooth and elastic.
  4. Cover the bowl with a plastic wrap and let the dough rise in a warm place for about 1 hour or until it has doubled in size.
  5. Punch down the dough and divide it into 12 equal portions. Roll each portion into a ball and place them in a greased brioche mold or a muffin tin.
  6. Cover the molds with a plastic wrap and let them rise again for about 30 minutes or until they have doubled in size.
  7. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  8. Brush the tops of the brioche with the egg wash.
  9. Bake the brioche for 25-30 minutes or until they are golden brown.
  10. Remove the brioche from the oven and let them cool on a wire rack.
