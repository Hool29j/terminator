Rye Bread

Rye bread is a type of bread made from rye flour, which is commonly consumed in Northern and Eastern Europe. It has a denser texture and a more robust, earthy flavor than bread made from wheat flour. Rye bread can be made with a mixture of rye flour and wheat flour, and may contain seeds or other grains.

It's often used to make sandwiches, particularly in Scandinavian countries where it's a popular staple. Rye bread is also known for its health benefits, as it's high in fiber and has a lower glycemic index than wheat bread.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups rye flour
  • 1/2 cups all-purpse flour
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp instant yeast
  • 1 tsp honey
  • 1 1/2 cups warm water

Step By Step

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the rye flour, all-purpose flour, salt, and instant yeast.
  2. In a separate bowl, mix the honey with the warm water until it dissolves.
  3. Add the honey mixture to the flour mixture and stir until a sticky dough forms.
  4. Cover the bowl with a clean dish towel and let the dough rise in a warm place for about 2 hours, or until it doubles in size.
  5. Preheat your oven to 450°F (230°C) and place a large baking dish filled with water on the bottom rack.
  6. Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and shape it into a round loaf.
  7. Place the loaf onto a greased baking sheet and let it rise again for about 30 minutes.
  8. Use a sharp knife to score the top of the loaf in a cross pattern.
  9. Bake the bread in the preheated oven for 30-40 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the bread sounds hollow when tapped.
  10. Let the bread cool on a wire rack before slicing and serving.
